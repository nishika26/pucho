# 21 Types of Disabilities Under the RPwD Act, 2016 (India)

**Domain:** Disability Rights / Government Support  
**Audience:** Parents, Caregivers, Educators, Healthcare Providers  

---

# Overview

The **Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act, 2016** is India’s primary disability rights law.

It expanded the officially recognized disability categories from **7 to 21**, creating a more inclusive legal framework for disability rights and support.

The Act ensures persons with disabilities receive equal opportunities in:

- Education
- Employment
- Healthcare
- Social participation
- Accessibility
- Legal protection

According to Census 2011, approximately **2.68 crore people** in India live with disabilities, representing around **2.21% of the population**.

The RPwD Act aligns India with the **United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD)**.

---

# Evolution of Disability Rights in India

Before RPwD 2016, India followed the **Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995**, which recognized only 7 disability categories.

The RPwD Act expanded this to 21 categories and shifted from a purely healthcare model to a broader social model of disability.

This means disability is understood not only as an impairment, but also as the result of barriers in society and infrastructure.

---

# Categories of Disability Under RPwD Act

The 21 disabilities are grouped into major categories:

- Physical disabilities
- Visual impairment
- Hearing and speech impairment
- Intellectual disabilities
- Mental illness
- Chronic neurological conditions
- Blood disorders
- Multiple disabilities

---

# 1. Physical Disabilities

Physical disabilities affect mobility or physical functioning.

---

## Locomotor Disability

Reduced ability to perform movement-related activities due to impairment in:

- Bones
- Joints
- Muscles
- Nervous system

---

## Leprosy Cured Person

Even after recovery from leprosy, individuals may experience:

- Loss of sensation
- Physical deformity
- Reduced mobility

These qualify under disability protections.

---

## Cerebral Palsy

A non-progressive neurological condition affecting:

- Movement
- Muscle coordination
- Posture

Usually caused by brain injury before, during, or shortly after birth.

---

## Dwarfism

A healthcare or genetic condition resulting in short stature.

---

## Muscular Dystrophy

A group of genetic disorders causing:

- Progressive muscle weakness
- Muscle degeneration

---

## Acid Attack Victims

Individuals with disfigurement or functional impairment due to acid attacks are protected under the Act.

---

# 2. Visual Impairment

---

## Blindness

Includes:

- Complete absence of sight
- Severe visual impairment even after correction

---

## Low Vision

Partial visual impairment where a person still uses remaining vision for daily tasks.

---

# 3. Hearing and Speech Impairment

---

## Deaf

Severe hearing loss affecting speech-frequency hearing.

---

## Hard of Hearing

Partial hearing loss affecting communication.

---

## Speech and Language Disability

Conditions affecting speech or language due to neurological or physical causes.

Examples include:

- Aphasia
- Laryngectomy-related impairment

---

# 4. Intellectual Disabilities

These affect learning, adaptive functioning, and independent living.

---

## Intellectual Disability

Significant limitations in:

- Intellectual functioning
- Adaptive behavior
- Daily living skills

---

## Specific Learning Disabilities (SLD)

Learning disabilities affecting specific academic skills.

Examples include:

- Dyslexia (reading)
- Dysgraphia (writing)
- Dyscalculia (math)

These often occur despite normal intelligence.

---

## Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

A neurodevelopmental condition affecting:

- Communication
- Social interaction
- Behavior

Symptoms often appear in early childhood.

---

# 5. Mental Illness

Mental illness includes substantial disorders affecting:

- Thinking
- Mood
- Memory
- Perception
- Orientation

Examples include:

- Schizophrenia
- Bipolar disorder

---

# 6. Chronic Neurological Conditions

---

## Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

A chronic condition where the protective covering of nerve cells is damaged.

This affects communication between the brain and body.

---

## Parkinson’s Disease

A progressive neurological condition affecting:

- Movement
- Balance
- Coordination

Common symptoms include tremors and rigidity.

---

# 7. Blood Disorders

---

## Haemophilia

A disorder where blood clotting ability is severely reduced.

---

## Thalassemia

An inherited blood disorder causing low hemoglobin levels.

---

## Sickle Cell Disease

A blood disorder where red blood cells become abnormally shaped.

This affects oxygen delivery and causes health complications.

---

# 8. Multiple Disabilities

Multiple disabilities involve more than one disability condition.

Example:

- Deafblindness

This category often requires specialized support and intervention.

---

# Why the Expansion to 21 Disabilities Matters

Expanding disability recognition improves access to:

- Government schemes
- Educational support
- Healthcare services
- Job reservations
- Financial assistance

It also improves:

- Early diagnosis
- Early intervention
- Social inclusion

This helps many individuals with previously overlooked conditions receive legal recognition and support.

---

# Rights Under RPwD Act

The RPwD Act guarantees equal rights and dignity for persons with disabilities.

---

## Right to Equality and Non-Discrimination

Persons with disabilities must be treated equally and protected from discrimination.

---

## Right to Education

Children with disabilities have access to:

- Free education
- Inclusive schooling
- Assistive support
- Reasonable accommodations

---

## Right to Employment

The Act provides:

- 4% reservation in government jobs
- Equal opportunity protections

---

## Right to Accessibility

Public spaces must be accessible, including:

- Buildings
- Transport
- Digital services

---

## Right to Social Security and Healthcare

Support includes:

- Financial assistance
- Rehabilitation
- Insurance
- Healthcare access

---

## Right to Dignity and Protection

The law protects against:

- Abuse
- Violence
- Exploitation
- Neglect

---

# Importance of Early Diagnosis and Intervention

Early diagnosis improves outcomes for many disabilities.

Benefits include:

- Faster support
- Better therapy outcomes
- Improved learning
- Greater independence

This is especially important for:

- Autism
- Intellectual disability
- Hearing impairment
- Developmental delays

Early intervention can significantly improve long-term quality of life.

---

# Challenges in India

Despite legal progress, several challenges remain.

---

## Lack of Awareness

Many families are unaware of disability categories and early warning signs.

---

## Social Stigma

Disabilities, especially invisible disabilities, are often misunderstood.

---

## Limited Access to Diagnosis

Rural and semi-urban regions often face shortages of:

- Specialists
- Screening facilities
- Therapists

---

## Delayed Intervention

Late diagnosis reduces effectiveness of support.

---

## Shortage of Trained Professionals

India still faces shortages of:

- Special educators
- Therapists
- Psychologists

---
