# Are There NGOs That Fund Therapy for Children?

**Domain:** Healthcare
**Audience:** Parents  

## Overview

Yes — NGOs, charitable trusts, government schemes, and CSR-funded programmes in India can help families cover the cost of children's developmental therapy.

Support may include:

- Full or partial therapy funding
- Subsidised therapy sessions
- Assistive devices
- Scholarships
- Disability-related financial benefits

Eligibility usually depends on:

- The child's developmental or disability-related needs
- Family income
- Required supporting documents

Families are often advised to apply to multiple funding sources at the same time.

---

## Funding Sources for Children's Therapy

## 1. Government Schemes

Government schemes are often a good starting point.

### Disability Certificate

A disability certificate is issued through:

- District Disability Rehabilitation Centres (DDRC)
- Government hospitals

This certificate helps families access several financial and support schemes.

---

### UDID Card (Unique Disability ID)

The UDID card provides a unified disability identity across India.

Benefits include:

- Easier access to disability-related schemes
- Simplified documentation
- Access to concessions and benefits

---

### ADIP Scheme

The **Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP)** scheme supports access to:

- Assistive devices
- Therapy aids
- Rehabilitation equipment

Support may be fully or partially subsidised.

---

### Niramaya Health Insurance Scheme

The **Niramaya Scheme**, under the National Trust, provides health insurance support for children with:

- Autism
- Cerebral palsy
- Intellectual disability
- Multiple disabilities

It can help reduce therapy-related financial burden.

---

## 2. NGOs and Charitable Trusts

Many NGOs and charitable organisations support therapy funding for children.

Support may include:

- Sponsoring therapy sessions
- Running subsidised therapy centres
- Sliding-scale fee models
- Funding specific therapy needs

Examples of funded services:

- Speech therapy
- Occupational therapy
- Physiotherapy
- Mobility aids
- Special education support

Many city-level and state-level NGOs offer such support.

---

## 3. CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) Programmes

Many companies in India support child-development and disability-related initiatives through CSR programmes.

Funding may be provided through:

- Direct therapy sponsorship
- Partner NGOs
- Community outreach programmes

CSR funding availability varies by region and organisation.

---

# How to Apply for Funding

## Step 1: Get a Developmental Assessment

Obtain a written developmental assessment from a qualified clinician.

This document helps establish:

- The child's needs
- Recommended therapies
- Frequency and duration of intervention

Most funding bodies ask for this.

---

## Step 2: Apply for Disability Certificate and UDID

If applicable, complete these early because they unlock many schemes and benefits.

---

## Step 3: Speak to Social Workers or Family Support Teams

Hospitals, therapy centres, and rehabilitation centres often have:

- healthcare social workers
- Family support teams

They can guide families toward relevant NGOs and schemes.

---

## Step 4: Apply to Multiple Sources

Do not wait for one application to be approved before starting another.

Applying to 2–3 funding sources at once improves chances of faster support.

---

# Important Considerations

## Funding Can Take Time

Approval processes may be slow.

Families should explore:

- Interim support options
- Sliding-scale therapy centres
- Subsidised services

This helps avoid delays in the child's therapy journey.

---

## Keep Documents Ready

Maintain a folder containing:

- Developmental assessment report
- Disability certificate
- UDID card
- Identity proof
- Income proof
- healthcare reports

Having these documents ready speeds up applications.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Do NGOs cover the full cost of therapy?

Some NGOs provide full funding, but many offer:

- Partial support
- Subsidised fees
- Sliding-scale pricing

Coverage depends on eligibility and available funding.

---

## What documents are usually required?

Common requirements include:

- Developmental assessment report
- Disability certificate (if applicable)
- UDID card (if applicable)
- Identity proof
- Income proof
- healthcare records

Requirements vary by organisation.

---

## Is a disability certificate necessary?

Not always for NGO funding.

However, it is highly useful because it unlocks access to many government schemes such as:

- ADIP
- Niramaya

---

# Key Takeaway

Therapy funding support for children is available in India through multiple channels:

- Government schemes
- NGOs
- Charitable trusts
- CSR programmes

Families should begin with a developmental assessment, gather essential documents, and apply to multiple funding sources simultaneously.

Early action improves access to both therapy and financial support.
