# Government Early Intervention in India: RBSK & DEIC

**Domain:** Family Access / Government Support  
**Audience:** Parents, Caregivers  

---

# Overview

India provides free government early-intervention services for children through:

- **RBSK (Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram)**
- **DEIC (District Early Intervention Centre)**

These programs support early screening, assessment, intervention, and referrals for children with developmental and health-related concerns.

Services are available through:

- Anganwadi centres
- Government schools
- Government hospitals
- District Hospitals

These services are free.

---

# What is RBSK?

**Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)** is a child health screening program under the **National Health Mission (NHM)**.

It screens children from birth to 18 years for the **4 Ds**:

- Defects at birth
- Deficiencies
- Diseases
- Developmental delays and disabilities

---

# Conditions Screened Under RBSK

RBSK screens for a wide range of health and developmental concerns.

Examples include:

- Developmental delays
- Speech delay
- Hearing problems
- Vision problems
- Cerebral palsy
- Down syndrome
- Autism-related concerns
- Congenital defects

Screening is free.

---

# How RBSK Works

Mobile health teams visit:

- Anganwadi centres
- Government schools
- Community health settings

They screen children and identify those who may need further assessment or intervention.

Children with identified concerns are referred to DEIC.

---

# What is DEIC?

**District Early Intervention Centre (DEIC)** is the specialist arm of the government early-intervention system.

DEICs are typically located at **District Hospitals**.

They provide:

- Detailed assessment
- Therapy
- Follow-up
- Specialist referrals

DEICs primarily support children flagged during RBSK screening, especially children under 6 years of age.

---

# Services Available at DEIC

A DEIC may offer assessment and support from multiple specialists.

This may include:

- Pediatrician
- Physiotherapist
- Occupational therapist
- Speech therapist
- Audiologist
- Psychologist
- Special educator

---

# Types of Support Provided

DEIC services may include:

- Developmental assessment
- Early intervention therapy
- Speech therapy
- Occupational therapy
- Physiotherapy
- Hearing assessment
- Vision assessment
- Psychological support
- Special education guidance

These services are provided free at government facilities.

---

# Role of Anganwadi and ICDS

Anganwadi centres are often the first point of contact for families.

They help with:

- Growth monitoring
- Child development tracking
- Early identification of concerns
- Referral to RBSK or DEIC

Anganwadi workers can guide families toward the next steps.

---

# Disability Certification and Government Support

DEIC and related government centres can also help families access disability-related support.

This includes guidance for:

- Disability Certificate
- UDID Card
- Government schemes
- Assistive devices

Support is linked to rights under the **Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016**.

---

# Referral Pathway for Specialist Care

Some children may require advanced treatment or surgery.

Examples include:

- Congenital heart defects
- Cleft lip and palate
- Hearing implants
- Specialist neurological care

In such cases, DEIC may refer the child to higher-level care centres.

Certain treatment costs may be covered under government schemes.

---

# How to Access RBSK and DEIC Services

Families can start through any of the following:

- Nearest Anganwadi centre
- Government school health program
- District Hospital
- Government health facility

Ask for:

- RBSK team
- DEIC services

---

# Documents to Carry

It is helpful to bring:

- Child’s immunisation card
- Previous medical records
- Assessment reports (if any)
- Referral notes (if available)

---

# Do You Need a Referral?

No.

Families can directly approach:

- Anganwadi centres
- District Hospitals
- Government health facilities

No referral is required to request screening or assessment.

---

# Signs You Should Seek Assessment Early

Parents should seek assessment if they notice:

- Delayed milestones
- Speech delay
- Poor eye contact
- Hearing concerns
- Vision concerns
- Behavioral concerns
- Developmental regression
- Conditions identified at birth

Early intervention improves outcomes.

---

# Benefits of Government Early Intervention

Key benefits include:

- Free screening
- Free assessment
- Free therapy services
- Access to specialist referrals
- Early identification of developmental concerns

Early support helps improve developmental outcomes and reduces long-term challenges.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Are RBSK and DEIC services free?

Yes.

Screening through RBSK and assessment and therapy through DEIC are provided free at government facilities.

---

## What is the difference between RBSK and DEIC?

**RBSK** is the screening program.

**DEIC** is the specialist intervention centre where children receive detailed assessment and therapy.

---

## Who can access these services?

Children from birth to 18 years can access RBSK screening.

DEIC commonly focuses on younger children, especially those needing early intervention.

---

## Do I need a referral?

No.

Families can directly approach government facilities.

---

## Can children receive government and private therapy together?

Yes.

Many families combine government early-intervention services with private therapy depending on their needs.

---

# Key Takeaway

India’s government early-intervention system provides free support for children through RBSK and DEIC.

These services help families access:

- Screening
- Early diagnosis
- Therapy
- Specialist referrals
- Government disability support

Parents should seek assessment early if they notice developmental concerns, as early intervention can significantly improve long-term outcomes.
