# Code on Wages, 2019

---

## Overview

The second National Commission on Labour recommended grouping existing labour laws into four or five labour codes based on functional areas. Consequently, the **Code on Wages, 2019** became one of the four labour codes enacted.

The Code on Wages, 2019 is a step toward advancing equity and labour welfare while ensuring enterprise sustainability. It standardizes definitions of key terms and streamlines procedures, reducing ambiguity and enabling faster, time-bound justice for employers.

The broader aim of these labour reforms is to drive economic growth through the generation of decent employment opportunities for all.

- **Notified on:** 8 August 2019  
- **Effective from:** 21 November 2025  

---

# Laws Subsumed Under the Code on Wages, 2019

The Code consolidates and rationalizes provisions from four existing labour laws:

- The Payment of Wages Act, 1936  
- The Minimum Wages Act, 1948  
- The Payment of Bonus Act, 1965  
- The Equal Remuneration Act, 1976  

It strengthens workers’ rights while promoting simplicity and uniformity in wage-related compliance for employers.

### Simplification Achieved

- Rules reduced from **163 → 58**
- Forms reduced from **20 → 6**
- Registers reduced from **24 → 2**

# Ensuring Fair and Minimum Wages

## Universalisation of Minimum Wages

Section 5 establishes a statutory right to minimum wages for all employees across both organized and unorganized sectors.

Previously, minimum wages applied only to scheduled employments, covering roughly **30% of the workforce**.

### Pro Worker Provisions

- Ensures minimum pay for every employee  
- Applies irrespective of industry, category, or nature of employment  
- Provides uniform legal safeguards nationwide  
- Benefits casual employees, daily wagers, and migrant labourers  
- Promotes social justice and reduces wage gaps  
- Improves income security and living standards  

### Pro Employment Provisions

- Encourages workforce participation  
- Improves job stability and retention  
- Boosts employment growth through fair compensation  

### Impact

- Reduces regional wage disparities  
- Promotes equity and social justice  
- Prevents wage undercutting by states  

---

## Introduction of Floor Wages

Section 9 read with Rule 11 introduces **floor wages**.

The Central Government fixes baseline wages based on minimum living standards, including:

- Food  
- Clothing  
- Shelter  
- Other essentials  

State governments must ensure their minimum wages are not below this floor.

### Pro Worker Provisions

- Prevents states from fixing wages below floor wage  
- Protects basic living needs  
- Promotes social and economic justice  
- Supports vulnerable and unorganized workers  

### Pro Growth Provisions

- Reduces labour migration caused by wage differentials  
- Prevents wage undercutting among states

# Fixing Minimum Wages

Minimum wage rates for:

- Time work  
- Piece work  

Can be fixed by:

- Hour  
- Day  
- Month  

These rates depend on:

- Skill level  
- Geographic area  
- Arduousness of work  

Governments revise wages at intervals not exceeding **5 years**.

### Pro Worker Provisions

- Links wages to skill and work difficulty  
- Protects low-skilled workers  
- Rewards skilled labour  
- Prevents exploitation  
- Improves living standards  
- Keeps wages aligned with inflation  

### Impact

- Recognizes skills and work intensity  
- Motivates upskilling  
- Improves job satisfaction  

---

# Re-defining Wage Components

For social security and benefit calculations, wages include:

- Basic Pay  
- Dearness Allowance  
- Retaining Allowance  

If allowances exceed **50% of total pay**, the excess is added to wages.

This affects:

- PF  
- Gratuity  
- Maternity Benefits  
- Bonus  

### Impact

- Expands social security coverage  
- Improves benefits for contractual and informal workers  
- Reduces exploitation  

---

# Working Hours

Section 13 read with Rule 6 limits normal working hours.

- Maximum **48 hours/week**  
- Maximum **12 hours/day** in flexible work arrangements  
- Includes rest intervals  
- Remaining days treated as paid holidays  

### Impact

- Protects employee health  
- Prevents overwork  
- Improves work-life balance  
- Enhances productivity

# Securing Fair and Consistent Wage Payments

## Ensuring Payment of Wages

Under Section 43, every employer must pay wages.

In case of failure, the establishment owner or responsible entity is liable.

---

## Timely Payment of Wages

Earlier, protections applied only to employees earning up to ₹24,000/month.

Now, protections apply to **all employees**, regardless of wage level.

This ensures:
- Fairness  
- Equal protection  
- Uniform wage safeguards  

---

## Time Limits for Wage Payment

Under Section 17:

- **Daily:** At shift end  
- **Weekly:** Before weekly holiday  
- **Fortnightly:** Within 2 days  
- **Monthly:** Within 7 days of next month  
- **Termination/Resignation:** Within 2 working days  

### Impact

- Prevents financial distress  
- Ensures timely income  
- Helps meet essential needs  

---

## Proof of Payment and Employment

Under Section 50(3) read with Rule 34, employers must provide wage slips:

- Electronic OR  
- Physical  

Must be given on or before wage payment.

### Benefits

- Improves transparency  
- Reduces disputes  
- Serves as proof of employment  
- Protects organized and unorganized workers  

---

# Payment of Annual Bonus

Bonus applies to employees:

- Drawing wages below government-notified threshold  
- Worked at least 30 days in accounting year  

Bonus rates:

- Minimum: **8.33%**
- Maximum: **20%**

### Benefits

- Promotes profit sharing  
- Boosts morale  
- Improves motivation and loyalty  

---

# Extension of Limitation Period

The limitation period for filing claims increased from:

**6 months–2 years → 3 years**

### Impact

- Gives employees more time to gather evidence  
- Improves access to justice  

---

# Minimum Time Rate Wages for Piece Work

Under Section 12, if minimum time rate is fixed for piece workers, wages must not fall below that rate.

### Pro Worker Provisions

- Prevents underpayment  
- Protects vulnerable workers  
- Supports sectors like manufacturing, textiles, construction  
- Ensures income stability  

---

# Payment of Overtime

Under Section 14, overtime must be paid at:

**At least twice the normal wage rate**

### Benefits

- Ensures fair compensation  
- Discourages exploitation  
- Protects rest rights  
- Provides additional income

# Decriminalization and Composition of Offences

### Impact

- Focuses on compliance rather than punishment  
- Encourages fair work environment  

## First-Time Offenders

The Code allows compounding of first-time offences not punishable with imprisonment.

Repeat offences within 5 years are excluded.

---

## Composition of Offences

Section 56 read with Rule 36 replaces criminal penalties for certain first-time offences with civil penalties.

- Fine-based offences can be compounded  
- Payment = 50% of maximum fine  

### Benefits

- Encourages compliance  
- Reduces fear-based enforcement  
- Protects employee interests  

---

# Growth-Inducing Provisions

- Uniform definitions of wages, worker, employee  
- Replaces Inspector Raj with tech-based inspections  
- Introduces random web-based inspections  
- Promotes cooperative compliance  
- Ensures faster justice  

---

# One Nation, One Code on Wage

Section 2 read with Rule 31 consolidates four wage laws into one.

Benefits:

- Uniform definitions  
- Simpler compliance  
- Reduced legal complexity  

---

# Inspector-cum-Facilitators

Under Section 51, Inspectors are replaced by **Inspector-cum-Facilitators**.

Role includes:

- Enforcement  
- Guidance  
- Awareness building  
- Advisory support  

### Benefits

- Promotes compliance  
- Improves worker welfare  
- Reduces adversarial inspections  

---

# Protection of Employer’s Assets

Section 64 safeguards deposits made by employers with the government for contract performance.

Such assets cannot be attached by court orders except for liabilities toward employees.

---

# Gender Inclusive Employment Policies

## Prohibition of Gender Discrimination

Under Section 3:

No discrimination based on gender, including transgender identity, in:

- Recruitment  
- Wages  
- Employment conditions  

Equal pay for equal work is ensured.

### Benefits

- Promotes workplace equality  
- Reduces wage disparities  
- Supports social and economic empowerment  

---

## Women’s Representation on Advisory Boards

Under Section 42:

At least **one-third members** of Central/State Advisory Boards must be women.

Boards advise on:

- Fixation of minimum wages  
- Wage revisions  
- Employment opportunities  

### Benefits

- Improves representation  
- Encourages inclusive policymaking  
- Supports balanced labour reforms
