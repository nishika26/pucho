# UDID Card in India: Complete Guide

**Domain:** Government Benefits / Disability Support  
**Audience:** Parents, Caregivers, Persons with Disabilities (PwDs)  

---

# Overview

The **UDID (Unique Disability Identity) Card** is India’s official identification card for Persons with Disabilities (PwDs).

It is issued by the **Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD)** under the **Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment**.

The UDID card helps individuals access:

- Government schemes
- Travel concessions
- Healthcare support
- Educational accommodations
- Financial assistance

It is valid across all states in India.

Official portal: https://www.swavlambancard.gov.in

---

# What is the UDID Card?

The UDID card is a digital identity card for persons with disabilities in India.

It serves as a single, nationally recognized document for disability-related services and benefits.

Before UDID, individuals often had to carry multiple certificates and documents to prove disability status across hospitals, schools, and government offices.

The UDID system simplifies this by providing one unified ID.

It functions similarly to Aadhaar, but specifically for disability-related services.

---

# Eligibility for UDID Card

The UDID card is available to Indian citizens with disabilities recognized under the **Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPWD) Act, 2016**.

Eligible disability categories include:

## Developmental Disabilities
- Autism
- Cerebral palsy
- Intellectual disability

## Physical Disabilities
- Locomotor disability
- Dwarfism
- Muscular dystrophy

## Sensory Disabilities
- Blindness
- Low vision
- Hearing impairment
- Speech and language disability

## Mental Health Conditions
Examples include:
- Schizophrenia
- Bipolar disorder

Eligibility depends on benchmark disability criteria.

## Neurological Conditions
Examples:
- Multiple sclerosis
- Parkinson’s disease

## Blood Disorders
Examples:
- Haemophilia
- Thalassemia
- Sickle cell disease

---

## Disability Requirement

To access benefits under most schemes, the applicant generally must have:

- At least **40% disability**
- Certification by a competent medical authority

The applicant must also be a permanent resident of India.

---

# Information Stored on the UDID Card

The card includes:

- Full name
- Date of birth
- Gender
- Address
- Type of disability
- Percentage of disability
- Unique ID number
- Medical assessment details
- Photograph
- Signature

---

# Card Colors and Disability Severity

Card color indicates disability percentage.

## White Card
- Below 40%

## Yellow Card
- 40% to 79%

## Blue Card
- 80% and above

Cards may also be:

- **Permanent** — for stable disabilities
- **Temporary** — requires renewal after reassessment

---

# Benefits of the UDID Card

## 1. Single Disability Identity

One card works across multiple disability-related services and schemes.

---

## 2. Access to Government Schemes

The card enables access to welfare schemes and financial assistance.

Examples include:

### Disability Pension
Examples by state:

- Delhi: up to ₹2,500/month
- West Bengal: ₹1,500/month
- Uttar Pradesh: ₹1,000/month

---

### ADIP Scheme

Provides assistive devices such as:

- Wheelchairs
- Hearing aids
- Braille kits

---

### Income Tax Benefits

Tax deduction under Section 80U:

- ₹75,000 for disability
- ₹1.25 lakh for severe disability

---

### Loans

Support via National Handicapped Finance Corporation:

- Loans up to ₹30 lakh
- Approx. 5% interest for self-employment

---

## 3. Travel Concessions

Cardholders may receive concessions in:

- Indian Railways
- Buses
- Public transport systems

Indian Railways concessions may range from 25%–75% depending on category.

---

## 4. Education Support

The UDID card helps students access accommodations such as:

- Extra exam time
- Scribes
- Accessible seating
- Learning support

---

## 5. Nationwide Validity

The card remains valid across India, even if the family relocates.

# How to Apply for a UDID Card

---

## Step 1: Visit Official Portal

Go to:

https://www.swavlambancard.gov.in

---

## Step 2: Start Application

Click:

**Apply for Disability Certificate and UDID Card**

You will choose one of the following:

- No disability certificate yet
- Existing certificate from UDID portal
- Older certificate from pre-UDID system

---

## Step 3: Fill Personal Details

Required details include:

- Name
- Mobile number
- Email
- Date of birth
- Gender
- Category
- Identity proof
- Address
- Disability details

Ensure details match official documents.

---

## Step 4: Enter Disability Details

Provide:

- Disability type
- Permanent or temporary disability
- Existing disability certificate details

---

## Step 5: Upload Documents

Common documents required:

- Passport-size photo
- Aadhaar or voter ID
- Address proof
- Disability certificate (if available)
- Medical reports
- Income certificate (if needed)
- Signature or thumbprint

---

## Step 6: Select Medical Authority

If no disability certificate exists, select an approved:

- Government hospital
- Medical board
- Assessment centre

A medical evaluation will be scheduled.

---

## Step 7: Submit Application

After submission:

- Application number is generated
- SMS confirmation is sent

Save the application number for tracking.

---

# What Happens After Applying?

Application review usually follows this flow:

Application Submission  
→ CMO/DMO verification  
→ Specialist assessment  
→ Medical board review  
→ Disability percentage assigned  
→ Certificate generation  
→ UDID card generation  

After approval:

- E-certificate becomes downloadable
- Digital card becomes available
- Physical card may be mailed

---

# Processing Time

Processing time varies.

Typical timelines:

- Few weeks
- Several months in some cases

Delays may occur depending on:

- State
- Medical board workload
- Documentation quality

Some applicants report delays of 3–6 months.

---

# How to Track Application Status

Use the **Track Your Application** feature on the portal.

You can track using:

- UDID Number
- Mobile Number
- Enrollment Number
- Aadhaar Number

---

# How to Download UDID Card

Go to:

https://www.swavlambancard.gov.in

Select:

**Download Certificate or Card**

Enter required details to download the digital copy.

UDID cards may also be available via DigiLocker.

---

# How to Update or Renew UDID Card

You can update details such as:

- Address
- Mobile number
- Aadhaar details
- Disability reassessment information

Renewal is required for temporary cards or expiring cards.

Updates and renewals are handled through the portal.

---

# Offline Application Support

Offline application is also available.

Support can be accessed through:

- District Welfare Officer
- District Social Welfare Officer
- Registration camps

These offices help with:

- Application filling
- Documentation
- Submission

---

# Important Update

As per government policy, the UDID card became mandatory for DEPwD schemes from **September 2024**.

This means many central disability-related schemes now require UDID for access.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is the UDID card the same as a disability certificate?

Not exactly.

The UDID card includes disability certificate information and serves as a unified disability identity.

---

## Can I apply if I already have an old disability certificate?

Yes.

Existing certificate holders can apply and may experience a faster process.

---

## Is the UDID card valid in another state?

Yes.

The UDID card is valid throughout India.

---

## Can I apply offline?

Yes.

Offline applications are allowed through district-level offices.

---

## What if my card is lost?

You can apply for a duplicate card through the official portal.

The UDID card is an important national identity document for persons with disabilities in India.

It simplifies access to:

- Government schemes

- Financial assistance

Families of children with disabilities should consider applying early, as many benefits and schemes now require a valid UDID card.
