# Disability Rights and Government Support in India

**Domain:** Government Benefits / Disability Support  
**Audience:** Parents, Caregivers, Persons with Disabilities (PwDs)

---

# Overview

India has established a broad legal and policy framework to support persons with disabilities (PwDs), focusing on:

- Accessibility
- Inclusion
- Education
- Healthcare
- Employment
- Financial support
- Skill development

According to Census 2011, India has approximately **2.68 crore persons with disabilities**, representing **2.21% of the population**.

India’s disability ecosystem includes:

- Legal protections
- Financial schemes
- Accessibility initiatives
- Assistive devices
- Skill development programs
- Employment support

---

# Legal Framework for Disability Rights

## Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (RPwD Act)

The RPwD Act is the primary disability rights law in India.

Key provisions:

- Recognizes **21 disability categories**
- Reservation in education and employment
- Equal opportunity protections
- Accessibility requirements
- Protection from discrimination

The Act promotes full participation and inclusion in society.

---

## National Trust Act, 1999

This Act focuses on welfare and support for individuals with:

- Autism
- Cerebral palsy
- Intellectual disability
- Multiple disabilities

It provides support for rehabilitation and social inclusion.

---

## Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) Act, 1992

The RCI regulates training and certification for rehabilitation professionals.

It oversees:

- Training standards
- Professional certification
- Rehabilitation education quality

---

## SIPDA Scheme

**Scheme for Implementation of Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act (SIPDA)** supports projects related to:

- Accessibility
- Awareness
- Inclusion
- Skill development

It provides financial support to states and institutions.

# Major Government Schemes

---

# Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan (Accessible India Campaign)

Launched in **2015**, this initiative focuses on improving accessibility in:

- Public infrastructure
- Transport systems
- Information and communication technology (ICT)

Goals:

- Barrier-free access
- Improved mobility
- Digital accessibility

---

## Sugamya Bharat App

The app helps PwDs access:

- Government schemes
- Accessibility information
- Public space accessibility ratings
- Grievance redressal

Features include:

- Accessibility mapping
- Scheme directory
- Complaint registration
- Multi-language support

---

# ADIP Scheme

**Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP)**

This scheme provides assistive devices and rehabilitation aids.

Examples include:

- Wheelchairs
- Hearing aids
- Cochlear implants
- Braille kits
- Mobility devices

Benefits:

- Improved independence
- Better mobility
- Reduced disability-related challenges

Corrective surgeries may also be covered.

---

# DDRS Scheme

**Deendayal Divyangjan Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS)** provides financial support to NGOs working in disability rehabilitation.

Support areas include:

- Education
- Training
- Rehabilitation
- Community inclusion

---

# NDFDC

**National Divyangjan Finance and Development Corporation (NDFDC)** supports economic empowerment of PwDs.

It provides:

- Loans for self-employment
- Microfinance support
- Income generation assistance

---

## Major Loan Schemes

### Divyangjan Swavalamban Yojana (DSY)
Provides concessional loans to individuals.

### Vishesh Microfinance Yojana (VMY)
Supports self-help groups and community-based financing.

---

# ALIMCO

**Artificial Limbs Manufacturing Corporation of India (ALIMCO)** manufactures assistive devices for PwDs.

Products include:

- Prosthetics
- Orthotics
- Hearing devices
- Mobility aids

ALIMCO also supports large-scale device distribution under government schemes.

---

# UDID Card

**Unique Disability Identity (UDID)** is a national disability identification system.

Benefits include:

- Centralized disability records
- Easier scheme access
- Uniform disability certification
- Reduced duplicate records

The UDID system allows:

- Online application
- Certificate issuance
- Updates and renewals
- Scheme integration

---

# Railway Divyangjan Card

This card helps persons with disabilities receive train travel concessions.

Benefits include:

- Discounted travel
- Simplified verification
- Disability-based concession access

---

### Divyangjan Rozgar Setu

# National Institutes and CRCs

India has specialized disability institutions providing:

- Rehabilitation services
- Therapy
- Professional training
- Awareness programs

Support is delivered through:

- National Institutes
- Composite Regional Centres (CRCs)

CRCs serve as regional outreach centers.

- Assistive technologies
- Inclusive education

The **Indian Sign Language Research and Training Centre (ISLRTC)** promotes ISL education and accessibility.

- ISL digital repository
- Sign language training
- Educational video resources
- Interpreter training

---

- 10,000+ dictionary terms

This app supports early disability screening in schools.

- Early identification
- Early intervention
- Better support planning

# Key Takeaway

India’s disability support ecosystem includes strong legal protections and multiple government schemes focused on:

- Accessibility
- Rehabilitation
- Education
- Financial assistance
- Employment
- Assistive devices
- Inclusion

Families and individuals should explore relevant schemes such as:

- ADIP
- UDID
- NDFDC
- DDRS
- Sugamya Bharat
- PM-DAKSH

These initiatives aim to improve independence, inclusion, and quality of life for persons with disabilities.
