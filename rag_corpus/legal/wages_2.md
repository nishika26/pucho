# Minimum Wage in India 2026: State-Wise Rates in USD

## Minimum Wage in India 2026 in INR & USD: Employer Guide

---

## TL;DR

- India has **no single national minimum wage**. Rates are set by:
  - State
  - Skill category
  - Industry
  - Zone  

- The central floor is **₹178/day (~$2.13)**, but metro state minimums run **4–6x higher**.

- The **Code on Wages, 2019** is now live:
  - Notified: **November 21, 2025**
  - Operational: **April 1, 2026**

- Every employee in India now has a statutory right to minimum wage.

- April 1, 2026 revision:
  - **Area A Unskilled:** ₹21,346/month (~$255)
  - **Highly Skilled:** ₹28,444/month (~$340)

### Key compliance rules

- Basic Pay + DA must be at least **50% of CTC**
- Salary must be paid by the **7th**
- Full and final settlement within **48 hours of exit**

---

# What is the minimum wage in India?

The minimum wage in India is the lowest legal pay an employer must pay a worker.

There is **no single national figure**.

It varies by:

- State
- Skill category:
  - Unskilled
  - Semi-skilled
  - Skilled
  - Highly skilled
- Industry
- Geographic zone

The central National Floor Level Minimum Wage is:

**₹178/day (~$2.13)**

---

## Factors affecting minimum wage

1. **State**  
   Each state sets its own rates.

2. **Skill category**  
   Pay rises with skill.

3. **Industry / employment type**  
   Construction, manufacturing, security etc. differ.

4. **Zone**  
   - Area A = Metro
   - Area B = Mid-tier
   - Area C = Rural/Other

---

# How is minimum wage calculated?

Formula:

```text
Minimum Wage = Base Wage + VDA (+ HRA where applicable)
```

### Steps

1. Classify worker  
2. Set base wage  
3. Adjust by zone & industry  
4. Add VDA  
5. Total wage

The Code replaced:

- Minimum Wages Act, 1948
- Payment of Wages Act, 1936
- Payment of Bonus Act, 1965
- Equal Remuneration Act, 1976

---

## Key Changes Under Labor Codes

- Universal minimum wage coverage
- National floor wage
- Uniform wage definition (50% rule)
- Salary payment by 7th
- 48-hour final settlement
- Fixed-term gratuity after 1 year
- State VDA revisions continue
- Gig/platform workers covered

# Who is covered?

Minimum wage now covers **all employees in India**.

### Covered
- Casual employees
- Salaried employees
- Part-time workers

### Not covered
- Self-employed workers
- Unpaid family workers
- Employers themselves

# What Counts as Wage?

## Included

- Basic Pay
- Dearness Allowance (DA)
- Retaining allowance
- Regular performance-linked pay

## Excluded

- HRA
- Conveyance
- Overtime
- Bonus
- PF contribution
- Gratuity

---

## Important Rule

If excluded allowances exceed **50% of CTC**, excess is treated as wages.

# Employer Cost Beyond Salary

Employers typically pay **15–20% above gross salary**.

Additional costs include:

- PF: 12%
- Gratuity: 4.81%
- ESI: 3.25% (if eligible)

---

# Overtime Rule

Under Section 14:

Overtime must be paid at **2x normal wage**.

Example:

Delhi skilled worker:
- Daily wage = ₹862
- Overtime day cost = **₹1,724**

# Minimum Wage vs Living Wage

Minimum wage = legal floor  
Living wage = actual cost of living

**₹26,000–₹35,000/month**
