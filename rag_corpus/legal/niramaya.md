# Niramaya Health Insurance Scheme (India)

**Domain:** Government Benefits / Disability Support  
**Audience:** Parents, Caregivers, Persons with Disabilities (PwDs)

---

# Overview

The **Niramaya Health Insurance Scheme** is a government-backed health insurance program under the **National Trust**.

It is designed to provide affordable healthcare coverage for individuals with developmental disabilities.

The scheme covers up to **₹1,00,000 per year** and helps families manage expenses related to:

- Hospitalization
- Therapies
- Outpatient care
- Alternative medicine
- Transport costs

The scheme is especially useful for families managing long-term developmental care.

---

# Key Features

- Annual coverage up to **₹1,00,000**
- No pre-medical tests required
- No age limit
- Available across India
- Covers multiple types of disability-related care

---

# Eligibility

The scheme is available to individuals covered under the **National Trust Act, 1999**.

Eligible conditions include:

- Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- Cerebral Palsy
- Intellectual Disability
- Multiple Disabilities

There is no age restriction.

Children and adults are both eligible.

---

# Required Documents

Common documents required for enrollment:

- Disability Certificate
- UDID Card or UDID enrollment number
- Address proof
- Aadhaar or other identity proof
- Income certificate (for BPL applicants)

---

# Coverage Details

Total annual coverage: **₹1,00,000**

| Coverage Type | Sub-limit | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Hospitalization | ₹55,000 | Surgery, hospital stays |
| OPD | ₹19,000 | Medicines, diagnostics |
| Therapies | ₹20,000 | Speech, occupational, physiotherapy |
| AYUSH | ₹4,000 | Ayurveda, homeopathy, etc. |
| Transport | ₹2,000+ | Travel for treatment |

# How to Apply

Applications are usually made through a **Registered Organisation (RO)** linked to the National Trust.

These are often:

- NGOs
- Disability support centres
- Local welfare organisations

---

## Step 1: Find a Registered Organisation

Locate a nearby Registered Organisation through the National Trust network.

---

## Step 2: Complete Application Form

The Registered Organisation usually helps with documentation and form filling.

---

## Step 3: Pay Registration Fee

### Initial Registration Fee

- BPL Families: ₹250
- Non-BPL Families: ₹500

In some cases, fees may be waived for legal guardians.

---

## Step 4: Verification

The Registered Organisation uploads documents for approval.

---

## Step 5: Approval and Card Generation

After approval, the beneficiary receives a **Niramaya Health Card**.

This serves as proof of enrollment.

---

# What is the Niramaya Health Card?

The Niramaya Health Card is the beneficiary’s official insurance identity under the scheme.

It is used for:

- Claim filing
- Verification
- Coverage tracking

---

# Information on the Health Card

The card typically contains:

- Beneficiary name
- Niramaya ID
- Date of birth
- Disability category
- Policy validity
- Photograph
- Registered Organisation details

---

# How Claims Work

Niramaya works as a **reimbursement model**.

This means:

1. Family pays medical expenses first
2. Claim is submitted later
3. Approved amount is reimbursed

---

# Claim Process

## Step 1: Visit Doctor or Hospital

Treatment can be taken at eligible hospitals or medical centers.

---

## Step 2: Keep All Bills

Save originals of:

- Bills
- Prescriptions
- Reports
- Discharge summaries

---

## Step 3: Fill Claim Form

Obtain the claim form from the relevant TPA.

Examples:
- MediAssist
- Raksha TPA

---

## Step 4: Submit Claim Within 30 Days

Claims should usually be submitted within **30 days** of treatment or discharge.

---

## Step 5: Track Claim Status

Claims can be tracked online using beneficiary details.

---

# Important Claim Tips

- Keep original bills
- Ensure all documents are signed and stamped
- Maintain updated bank details
- Therapy prescriptions may require renewal every 6 months

---

# Renewal Process

Niramaya follows the financial year cycle.

Policy validity typically ends on:

**March 31**

Renewal is required every year.

---

# Renewal Fees

- BPL Families: ₹50
- Non-BPL Families: ₹250

Renew before expiry to avoid loss of coverage.

---

# Common Reasons for Claim Rejection

Understanding these can prevent reimbursement delays.

---

## Incomplete Documents

Missing bills, reports, or prescriptions can cause rejection.

---

## Late Submission

Claims submitted after the allowed window may be rejected.

---

## Treatment Not Covered

Non-covered treatments may not be reimbursed.

Examples:
- Cosmetic procedures
- Experimental treatments

---

## Bills Not in Beneficiary’s Name

Bills should match beneficiary details.

---

## Expired Health Card

Claims during inactive coverage periods are not accepted.

---

## Mismatch in Personal Details

Differences in:

- Name
- DOB
- ID number
- Bank details

can cause verification failures.

---

## Missing Signatures or Stamps

Medical documents should be properly signed and stamped.

---

## Claim Above Category Limits

Each category has sub-limits.

Expenses beyond limits may not be reimbursed.
