# Introduction

The Government of India remains committed to promoting equality and social justice through inclusive policies and programmes that uplift all sections of society. The Ministry of Labour and Employment aims to enhance employment opportunities sustainably by providing decent working conditions and quality of life.

The second National Commission on Labour recommended grouping existing labour laws into four or five Labour Codes on a functional basis. Consequently, the **Code on Wages, 2019** is one of the four enacted labour codes.

The Code on Wages, 2019 advances equity and labour welfare while ensuring enterprise sustainability. It standardizes key definitions and streamlines procedures, reducing ambiguity and ensuring faster, time-bound justice for employers.

The broader aim of these labour reforms is to drive economic growth through decent employment opportunities for all.

---

# The Code on Wages, 2019: Laws Subsumed

The Code amalgamates four wage-related labour laws:

- The Payment of Wages Act, 1936
- The Minimum Wages Act, 1948
- The Payment of Bonus Act, 1965
- The Equal Remuneration Act, 1976

It balances worker protection with ease of compliance for employers.

---

## Key Benefits

- Protects workers through fair wages and social security
- Prevents exploitation
- Supports women through equal pay and representation
- Promotes productivity and labour welfare
- Strengthens economic growth and employment generation

# Universalisation of Minimum Wages

Section 5 establishes a statutory right to minimum wages for all employees in both organised and unorganised sectors.

Earlier, minimum wages covered only scheduled employments (~30% of workforce).

# Introduction of Floor Wages

Section 9 introduces floor wages.

The Central Government fixes baseline wages based on minimum living standards.

State governments cannot fix wages below this floor.

# Fixing Minimum Wages

Minimum wages may be fixed:

- Hourly
- Daily
- Monthly
- Time work
- Piece work

Factors include:

- Skill level
- Geographical area
- Arduousness of work

Revision occurs generally every **5 years**.

# Re-defining Wage Components

## Impact

Contractual and informal workers now receive a fairer wage structure and stronger social security.

For benefits and social security calculations, wages include:

- Basic Pay
- Dearness Allowance (DA)
- Retaining Allowance

If allowances exceed **50% of total compensation**, excess gets added to wages.

This affects:

- PF
- Gratuity
- Maternity benefits
- Bonus

# Stating Working Hours

## Impact

- Protects employee health
- Prevents exploitation
- Promotes work-life balance
- Improves productivity

Section 13 limits working hours:

- Max **48 hours/week**
- Max **12 hours/day** (where flexible work exists)

Remaining days are paid holidays.

# Ensuring Payment of Wages

Section 43 mandates employers to pay wages.

Employers are directly responsible for unpaid wages.

---

# Timely Payment of Wages

Timely payment and deduction protections now apply to **all employees**, regardless of wage ceiling.

Earlier this applied only to employees earning ≤ ₹24,000/month.

---

# Payment Timelines

Section 17 requires wage payment as follows:

- Daily → End of shift
- Weekly → Before weekly holiday
- Fortnightly → Within 2 days
- Monthly → Within 7 days of next month
- Termination/resignation → Within 2 working days

---

# Proof of Payment and Employment

Section 50(3) requires wage slips:

- Electronic or physical
- Before wage payment

Benefits:

- Transparency
- Fewer disputes
- Documentary proof of employment

---

# Payment of Annual Bonus

Applicable to employees:

- Drawing wages within prescribed limits
- Worked at least 30 days in accounting year

Bonus range:

- Minimum: **8.33%**
- Maximum: **20%**

Benefits:

- Profit sharing
- Higher morale
- Better motivation

---

# Extension of Limitation Period

Claim filing limitation increased to **3 years**.

Earlier: **6 months–2 years**

This gives employees more time to seek justice.

---

# Minimum Time Rate Wages for Piece Work

Section 12 protects piece-rate workers.

Employers must pay at least minimum time rate if fixed.

# Decriminalization and Composition of Offences

## Impact

- Focus shifts from punishment to compliance
- Encourages fair work environments

---

# First-Time Offenders

First-time offences not punishable by imprisonment may be compounded.

Exception:
- Same offence repeated within 5 years

---

# Composition of Offences

Section 56 replaces criminal penalties with civil penalties for fine-only offences.

Compounding amount:
- **50% of maximum fine**

Benefits:

- Encourages compliance
- Reduces fear-driven environments
- Protects employee interests

# One Nation, One Code on Wage

Section 2 consolidates four wage laws into one code.

Uniform definitions include:

- Wages
- Worker
- Employee

---

# Inspector-cum-Facilitators

Section 51 replaces Inspector with Inspector-cum-Facilitator.

Role includes:

- Enforcement
- Guidance
- Awareness
- Advisory support

---

# Protection of Employer’s Assets

Section 64 safeguards amounts deposited with government for contract performance.

Such amounts are protected from court attachment except employee-related liabilities.

# Prohibition of Gender Discrimination

Section 3 prohibits discrimination based on gender, including transgender identity.

No discrimination in:

- Recruitment
- Wages
- Employment conditions

Ensures:

- Equal pay for equal work
- Workplace fairness
- Economic empowerment

---

# Women’s Representation on Advisory Boards

Section 42 mandates:

**One-third members of Central/State Advisory Boards must be women**

Benefits:

- Inclusive policy making
- Balanced employment policies
- Greater opportunities for women
