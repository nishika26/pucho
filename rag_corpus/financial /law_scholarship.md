## 1. Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship

This scholarship supports deserving students from low-income households by helping cover ongoing educational expenses.

### Eligibility

- Students above the **80th percentile** in Class XII in their respective board/stream.
- Must pursue **regular degree programs** (not distance/correspondence).
- Must be enrolled in recognized institutions.
- Must not already receive benefits from another scholarship scheme.
- Annual parental/family income must be **≤ ₹4.5 lakh**.
- Scholarship can continue if student changes colleges (provided new college has valid AISHE code).
- Minimum requirements for renewal:
  - **50% marks in annual exam**
  - **75% attendance**
  - No disciplinary complaints/ragging cases

Applications are submitted through the **National Scholarship Portal (NSP)**.

### Scholarship Amount

- Graduation: **₹12,000/year** (first 3 years)
- Postgraduation: **₹20,000/year**
- 4th & 5th year integrated programs: **₹20,000/year**

> Note: For academic year 2021–22 renewals, rate remained ₹10,000/year.

## 2. Aditya Birla Scholarship Programme

Started in 1999, this scholarship supports top students studying at elite institutions.

### Scholarship Package

- **Law:** ₹1,80,000 per year or actual fees (whichever is lower)

### Eligibility

Applicable to students admitted in:

- NALSAR University of Law
- NLSIU Bangalore
- Gujarat National Law University
- WB National University of Juridical Sciences
- National Law University Jodhpur

### Selection Process

- Application review
- Academic + extracurricular evaluation
- Essay shortlisting
- Interview round

Final shortlisted candidates receive:
- Lodging
- Travel reimbursement
- Small allowance for interviews in Mumbai

## 3. Fully Funded Science & Law School Scholarship

Offered by **Affidavit Institute of Judicial Practice**.

Supports students studying in science or law programs at institutions ranked in top 1000 QS rankings.

### Eligibility

- Master’s candidate should be under 30 years
- Previous education must be in English
- Must have admission/pre-admission from eligible institution
- Must provide 2 recommendation letters
- Minimum academic score: **75%**
- For research programs, research proposal required

### Scholarship Amount

**Fully Funded**

### Application Process

Applicants must submit CV to:

`finaid@afidavit.com`

Required CV details:
- University name + QS ranking
- Program duration
- Enrollment date
- Academic scores
- Age
- Contact details

## 4. Nirankari Rajmata Scholarship Scheme

Merit-cum-means scholarship by **Sant Nirankari Charitable Foundation**.

### Eligibility

- Admission to recognized law program
- Minimum **90% in Class XII**
- Must qualify law entrance exam
- Annual family income **≤ ₹3.5 lakh**

Income proof required.

### Scholarship Amount

- Covers tuition fees only
- Maximum benefit: **₹75,000/year**

### Required Documents

- Application form
- Mark sheets from Class X onward
- Class X & XII certificates
- Income certificate
- Institute certificate
- Affidavit
- Semester results
- Fee receipts
- Bank details
- Admission letter
- ID proof

---

## 5. GEV Memorial Merit Scholarship

Scholarship for deserving law students in India.

### Eligibility

- Indian citizen
- Enrolled in LLB/LLM
- Must have appeared for law entrance exams:
  - CLAT
  - LSAT
  - AILET
- Minimum **60% in Class X and XII**
- Family income **≤ ₹10 lakh/year**
- Must participate in mentorship program

### Scholarship Amount

**₹50,000 – ₹2,00,000/year**

Additional benefit:
- Mentorship opportunities

## 6. PNB Housing Finance Scholarship for Law

Offered by **PNB Housing Finance Limited**.

### Eligibility

- Students in 3rd/4th year of BA-LLB
- Applicable for students at **Army Institute of Law, Mohali**
- Minimum **65% marks**
- Family income **≤ ₹8 lakh/year**

Check official website for updated scholarship amount and deadlines.

---

## 7. Quill Foundation Scholarship

Scholarship + mentoring for aspiring law students from financially weaker backgrounds.

### Eligibility

- Minimum **60% marks in 10+2**
- Must not opt for self-financed seats
- Financial need required

Focus areas:
- Legal education
- Social justice
- Community rights work

---

## 8. Post-Matric Scholarship for Minorities (MOMA)

Scholarship by **Ministry of Minority Affairs (MOMA)**.

Supports minority students facing financial difficulties.

### Scholarship Categories

#### Pre-Matric Scheme
- Class 1–10
- Family income < ₹1 lakh
- Minimum 50% marks

#### Post-Matric Scheme
- Class 11 to PhD
- Family income < ₹2 lakh
- Minimum 50% marks

#### Merit-cum-Means Scholarship
- Graduate/Postgraduate technical or professional courses
- Family income < ₹2.5 lakh
- Minimum 50% marks

---

### Required Documents

- Passport-size photograph
- Course fee receipt
- Income certificate
- Community certificate
- Aadhaar card
- Domicile certificate
- Previous mark sheet
- Bank passbook
- Declaration form

### Selection Criteria

Selection based on:
- Financial need
- Academic merit (for technical/professional programs)
- Preference to older applicants
- Preference to students in reputed institutions
