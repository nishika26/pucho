# Scholarships for EWS Students in India: Eligibility, Benefits and Documents Required

## Scholarships for EWS Students

Pursuing higher education can be challenging for students belonging to the Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) due to increasing academic expenses and limited financial resources. To address this issue, the Government of India, educational institutions, and private organisations implement numerous scholarship schemes aimed at providing quality education to eligible EWS students.

Some of the popular scholarships for EWS students in India are:

- National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS)
- PM YASASVI Schemes for School and College Education
- Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships for College and University Students
- Post-Matric Scholarship for EBC students

Each scheme follows distinct eligibility criteria, income criteria, selection procedures, and fund disbursement structures, which applicants must review carefully before applying.

# Scholarships for EWS Students in India – Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for EWS scholarships vary across schemes but generally focus on:

- Family income limits  
- Academic performance  
- Category requirements  
- Institutional recognition  

Applicants must carefully check scheme-specific conditions before applying.

---

## 1. National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMS)

- Parental income from all sources must not exceed **₹3,50,000 per annum**
- Minimum **55% marks in Class 7**
- **5% relaxation** for SC/ST students
- Must be studying as a regular student in:
  - Government school
  - Government-aided school
  - Local body school
- Students studying in:
  - Navodaya Vidyalayas (NVS)
  - Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVS)
  - Residential schools  
  are **not eligible**
- Reservation applies as per state government norms

---

## 2. PM YASASVI Top Class Education in Schools (OBC, EBC & DNT)

- Supports meritorious students from **Class 9–12**
- Only for students belonging to:
  - OBC
  - EBC
  - DNT
- Must study in a top-class school identified under the scheme
- Annual household income must be below **₹2.5 lakh**
- Selection is merit-based
- State-wise scholarship allocation depends on OBC population data

---

## 3. PM YASASVI Top Class Education in College (OBC, EBC & DNT)

- Applicant must belong to:
  - OBC
  - EBC
  - DNT
- Annual family income up to **₹2.5 lakh**
- Must secure admission in a full-time prescribed course in a notified institution
- Scholarships are limited by institution/course slots
- If applications exceed slots:
  - Higher-ranked students get preference
- Tie-breaker:
  - Lower family income gets preference
- **30% slots reserved for girls**
- Maximum **two siblings per family**
- Preference given to OBC students during selection

---

## 4. Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships for College and University Students

- Must be above the **80th percentile in Class 12**
- Must pursue a regular degree course
- Distance, correspondence, and diploma courses are not eligible
- Institution must be AICTE-approved or recognized by relevant authority
- Cannot avail another scholarship simultaneously
- Annual family income must not exceed **₹4.5 lakh**
- For renewal:
  - Minimum **50% marks**
  - Minimum **75% attendance**
- Applications submitted via **National Scholarship Portal (NSP)**

---

## 5. Post-Matric Scholarship for EBC Students

- Open to General category students only  
  (excluding SC, ST, OBC)
- Employed students with family income up to **₹1 lakh**:
  - Eligible for non-refundable fee reimbursement only
- Unemployed students with family income up to **₹1 lakh**:
  - Fully eligible
- Income certificate mandatory
- Applicable only for recognized post-matric courses in government institutions
- CPL and aviation courses are not eligible
- Distance learners eligible for:
  - Non-refundable fee reimbursement
  - ₹900 annual book allowance
- Only **two boys per family**
- No limit for girl students
- Cannot hold multiple scholarships simultaneously

# Scholarships for EWS Students in India – Benefits

Scholarships for EWS students provide financial support for:

- Tuition fees
- Maintenance allowance
- Academic expenses
- Hostel/living costs

Benefits vary by scheme.

---

## NMMS Benefits

- Financial assistance of **₹12,000 per year**

---

## PM YASASVI School Benefits

- Covers tuition fees, hostel charges, and school-related expenses
- Up to:
  - **₹75,000/year** for Classes 9–10
  - **₹1,25,000/year** for Classes 11–12

---

## PM YASASVI College Benefits

- Tuition and non-refundable charges covered
- Maximum reimbursement:
  - **₹2 lakh/year** in private institutions
  - **₹3.72 lakh/year** for private flying clubs

Additional benefits:

- Living allowance: **₹3,000/month**
- Book allowance: **₹5,000/year**
- One-time laptop assistance: **₹45,000**

---

## Central Sector Scholarship Benefits

- **₹12,000/year**

### Post Graduation

# Documents Required for Scholarships for EWS Students in India

Common documents required include:

- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Voter ID Card
- Passport (if applicable)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR)
- Salary slips of earning family members
- Bank statements
- Income certificate
- Class 10 and 12 marksheets
- Degree certificates
- Admission confirmation letter
- Income certificate from Tehsildar or Revenue Officer
- Caste certificate (if applicable)
- Disability certificate (if applicable)
- Domicile certificate (if applicable)
