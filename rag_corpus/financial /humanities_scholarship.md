## Types of Scholarships for Humanities Students

Scholarships for humanities students are available for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research-level students.

They can be categorized into the following types:

---

### 1. Merit-Based Scholarships

These scholarships reward students with strong academic performance.

Examples:

- **UPES Humanities Scholarships**
  - Up to **30% merit scholarships**
  - For B.A. Honors programs in:
    - English
    - History
    - Political Science
  - Based on:
    - Class 12 scores
    - CUET scores
    - SAT scores

Many universities in India and abroad also provide merit scholarships based on academic achievements.

---

### 2. Need-Based Scholarships

These scholarships support students from economically weaker backgrounds.

Example:

- **Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Protsahan Yojana (PM-USP)**  
  Provides financial assistance based on:
  - Income level
  - Academic merit

---

### 3. Government and Institutional Scholarships

Several government and academic institutions provide scholarships for humanities students.

Examples:

- **Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR)**
  - Scholarships for Indian and international students
  - Supports:
    - Undergraduate
    - Postgraduate
    - PhD programs

Universities offering financial aid include:

- Amrita University  
- Shiv Nadar University  
- Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE)  

---

### 4. International Scholarships

Students interested in studying humanities abroad can apply for international scholarships.

Examples:

- **Arts and Humanities Deans Global Talent Scholarship (UK)**
  - £10,000 toward tuition

- **Fulbright Foreign Student Scholarship (USA)**

- **Inlaks Foundation Scholarship**
  - Supports students in:
    - Social Sciences
    - Humanities
    - Law
    - Fine Arts

---

### 5. Research and Fellowship Scholarships

Designed for postgraduate and doctoral students.

These scholarships support:
- Thesis work
- Research projects
- Academic inquiry

Example:

- **University of Glasgow PhD Scholarships**
  - Fully funded
  - Covers:
    - Tuition fees
    - Living stipend

# Eligibility Criteria for Humanities Scholarships

Eligibility varies by scholarship, but common criteria include:

---

### Academic Performance

Most scholarships require strong academic performance.

Typical minimum requirement:
- **60–75% marks** in previous qualifying exams

Examples:
- Class 12
- Bachelor’s degree
- Master’s degree

---

### Field of Study

Applicants must be pursuing or planning to pursue humanities-related disciplines such as:

- History  
- Literature  
- Political Science  
- Sociology  
- Philosophy  
- Fine Arts  

---

### Nationality or Residency

Some scholarships are for:

- Indian nationals
- Indian residents
- International students coming to India
- Indians studying abroad

---

### Admission Status

Many scholarships require confirmed admission to a recognized university or institution.

---

### Age Limits

Some scholarships have age restrictions.

Example:
- **Inlaks Foundation Scholarship**
  - Applicants must be born after **January 1, 1995** (for 2025 cycle)

---

### Entrance Exams

Some institutions require scores from exams such as:

- CUET  
- Other entrance tests  

---

### Other Requirements

Additional criteria may include:

- Financial need  
- Extracurricular achievements  
- Research proposals  
- Recommendation letters  

Example: **Inlaks Scholarship**
- Minimum **65% marks**
- Proof of admission
- Indian residency required

# How to Apply for Humanities Scholarships

Applying for scholarships requires planning and careful preparation.

---

## Step 1: Research Scholarships

Find scholarships matching:

- Academic level
- Subject area
- Nationality

Sources to explore:

- University websites
- Government portals
- Scholarship databases

Examples:
- UPES
- Amrita University
- ICCR
- PM-USP

---

## Step 2: Check Eligibility

Ensure you meet all criteria, including:

- Academic qualifications
- Field of study
- Admission status
- Age requirements

---

## Step 3: Prepare Documents

Common documents include:

- Academic transcripts and certificates
- Proof of admission/application
- ID proof
- Nationality/residency proof
- Recommendation letters
- Statement of Purpose (SOP)
- Scholarship essay
- Entrance exam results (if required)

---

## Step 4: Apply Online or Offline

Many scholarships use online portals.

Example:

- **ICCR A2A Portal**
  - Fully digital scholarship application process

Some scholarships may require:
- Paper applications
- Email submission

---

## Step 5: Submit Before Deadline

Track deadlines carefully.

Example:
- UPES scholarship deadline may close around **February 28 annually**

---

## Step 6: Follow Up

After submission:

- Check application status
- Respond to scholarship offices if needed
- Prepare for interviews or assessments

# Tips to Maximize Scholarship Success

- **Start early**  
  Scholarship applications take time.

- **Maintain strong academics**  
  Merit-based scholarships heavily value grades.

- **Write strong essays**  
  Clearly explain:
  - Your passion for humanities
  - Career goals
  - Financial need

- **Get strong recommendations**  
  Choose mentors who know your strengths well.

- **Apply widely**  
  Don’t depend on one scholarship.

- **Follow instructions carefully**  
  Small mistakes can lead to rejection.

---

Applying strategically can significantly improve your chances of winning scholarships and reducing the financial burden of higher education in humanities.
