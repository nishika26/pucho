# Scholarships for Engineering Students – Eligibility Criteria (Funded by Central Government)

Students enrolled in engineering courses can avail of numerous scholarships provided by the central government. Some popular scholarships include the **AICTE Swanath Scholarship Scheme** and **Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship for College and University Students**.

## AICTE Swanath Scholarship Scheme

- Must be an Indian citizen  
- A ward who lost one or both parents due to COVID-19  
- A ward of Armed Forces or Central Paramilitary Forces personnel martyred in action (Shaheed)  
- A ward of a person with disability (PwD) having 80% or more disability caused by an accident  
- A ward of a parent suffering from a critical or life-threatening illness  
- Enrolled full-time in 1st, 2nd, 3rd, or 4th year of a regular degree/diploma program at an AICTE-approved institution  
- Family income below ₹8,00,000 per annum  

## AICTE Saksham Scholarship Scheme for Specially Abled Students

- Must be an Indian national  
- Benchmark disability of 40% or above  
- Enrolled in 1st year or 2nd year (via lateral entry) of degree/diploma at AICTE-approved institution  
- Family income below ₹8,00,000 per annum  

## AICTE Pragati Scholarship Scheme for Girl Students

- Female Indian national  
- Enrolled in 1st year of diploma/degree at AICTE-approved institution  
- Or enrolled in 2nd year through lateral entry  
- Family income below ₹8,00,000 per annum  

## Scholarship for Top Class Education for Students With Disabilities

- Indian citizen  
- Minimum 40% disability under RPwD Act, 2016  
- Pursuing graduate/postgraduate/diploma course from recognized institution  
- Family income below ₹8,00,000  

## Post-Matric Scholarship for Students With Disabilities

- Indian citizen  
- Studying from Class 11 to postgraduation  
- Disability of 40% or more  
- Family income below ₹2,50,000  

## Central Sector Scheme of Scholarship

- Indian national  
- Passed Class 12 with minimum 80% marks  
- Pursuing UG/PG in professional field from recognized institution  
- Family income below ₹4,50,000  

## Khorana Program for Scholars

- Indian citizen  
- Enrolled in recognized institution in India  
- Pre-final year student in engineering or related fields  
- Minimum CGPA of 8.0 or 80% marks  

## S.N. Bose Scholars Program

- Indian citizen pursuing bachelor’s/master’s degree in India  
- Open to engineering sciences, chemical sciences, physical sciences, etc.

# Scholarships for Engineering Students – Eligibility Criteria (Funded by State Government)

## Swami Vivekananda Merit Cum Means Scholarship (West Bengal)

- Resident of West Bengal  
- Minority/economically backward family  
- Pursuing engineering in AICTE-approved institution  
- Minimum marks:
  - UG 1st year: 60% in Higher Secondary  
  - UG 2nd year lateral entry: 60% in Diploma  
  - PG: 55% in Graduation  
- Family income ≤ ₹2,50,000  
- Must not hold another government scholarship  

## Post-Matric Scholarship for Persons with Disability (Maharashtra)

- Resident of Maharashtra  
- Disability of 40% or more  
- Studying from Class 11 to Ph.D.  
- Admitted through CAP  

## Technical Education Scholarships for Diploma Students (Himachal Pradesh)

- Resident of Himachal Pradesh or child of HP govt employee  
- Enrolled in AICTE-approved diploma/engineering course  
- Minimum 60% in qualifying exam  
- Family income ≤ ₹1,50,000  

## Nagaland State Merit Scholarship

- Resident of Nagaland  
- Post-matric level in recognized institution  
- Minimum marks:
  - UG: 80% in Class 10/12/Diploma  
  - PG: 70% in Graduation  

## Rajasthan Yuva Vikas Prerak Internship Program (RYVP)

- Age 18–35 years  
- Graduates/Postgraduates in Engineering, healthcare, Agriculture, Law, etc.  
- Strong communication and IT skills  

## NEC Merit Scholarship

- Indian citizen and permanent resident of Northeastern state  
- Enrolled in diploma/degree/PG/M.Phil./Ph.D.  
- Minimum 60% marks  
- Family income < ₹8,00,000

# Scholarships for Engineering Students – Eligibility Criteria (Private Funded)

## Bharti Airtel Scholarship Program

- Indian students admitted in 2026–27 to top 50 NIRF engineering institutes  
- Courses: ECE, IT, CS, Data Science, Aerospace, AI, IoT, ML, Robotics  
- Family income ≤ ₹8.5 lakh  
- Preference for girls, disabled, single/no parent, transgender students  

## Aspire Scholarship Program

- First-year B.Tech students at premier institutes  
- Family income ≤ ₹6,00,000  

## Legrand Empowering Scholarship Program

- Open to differently-abled girls, LGBTQ+, Covid-affected students, single-parent/orphan students  
- Admission in B.Tech, B.E, B.Arch, BBA, B.Com, or B.Sc  
- 60% in Class 10 & 12  
- Family income ≤ ₹5,00,000  

## Panasonic Ratti Chhatr Scholarship

- First-year B.E./B.Tech students at IITs  
- 75%+ in Class 12  
- Family income ≤ ₹8,00,000  

## HDFC Bank Parivartan ECSS Programme

- Indian students pursuing graduation/professional courses  
- Minimum 55% in previous exam  
- Family income ≤ ₹2,50,000
