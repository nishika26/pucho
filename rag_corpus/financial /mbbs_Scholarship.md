# Why healthcare Students Need Scholarships More Than Most

A student pursuing a **B.Sc. Nursing** or **B.Pharm** can budget around **₹2 lakh to ₹6 lakh** for the full course at a government college. An **MBBS student at a private institution** can face fees of **₹40 lakh to ₹1 crore** across the 5.5-year programme.

Even at government colleges, where fees are regulated, costs such as:

- Hostel fees  
- Clinical equipment  
- Textbooks  
- Examination fees  
- Living expenses  

accumulate significantly over time.

Scholarships offer vital financial support by covering tuition fees, living expenses, or both. Some even provide **full funding**, allowing students to focus on academics without financial stress.

The **10 schemes below** span government, corporate, and foundation sources.

Some are exclusive to MBBS students, while others cover:

- Nursing  
- Pharmacy  
- BDS  
- BAMS  
- BHMS  
- Physiotherapy  
- Parahealthcare courses

# 10 Scholarships for MBBS, Nursing and Pharmacy Students in India

---

## 1. GSK Scholars Programme

**Amount:** Up to ₹1,00,000 per year for 4.5 years  
**Who it is for:** First-year MBBS students in government healthcare colleges  

The **GSK Scholars Programme 2025-26** supports meritorious healthcare students facing financial constraints while pursuing MBBS in India.

### Eligibility
- Enrolled in first-year MBBS at a government healthcare college  
- Minimum 65% in Class 12  
- Annual family income below ₹6,00,000  
- Indian nationals only  

### Covers
- Tuition fees  
- Examination fees  
- Textbooks  
- Hostel charges  
- Food expenses  
- Seminars  
- Stationery  
- Online learning tools  
- Internet/data bills  

**Apply at:** `ffe.org/ffe-gsk-scholarship-program`

---

## 2. ONGC Scholarship to Meritorious Students

**Amount:** ₹48,000 per year (₹4,000/month)  
**Who it is for:** First-year MBBS, engineering, and MBA students from SC, ST, OBC, and General EWS categories  

### Eligibility
- First-year MBBS student in NMC-approved institution  
- Minimum 60% in Class 12  
- Family income below:
  - ₹2 lakh (General/OBC)  
  - ₹4.5 lakh (SC/ST)  
- Age below 30 years  

### Additional Info
- 50% scholarships reserved for female candidates  
- Total scholarships awarded: **2,000 per year**

**Apply at:** `ongcscholar.org`

---

## 3. Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships (NSP)

**Amount:**  
- ₹20,000/year (UG)  
- ₹25,000/year (PG)

**Who it is for:** MBBS, BDS, nursing, pharmacy, and professional course students  

### Eligibility
- Top 80th percentile in Class 12 board exam  
- Annual family income below ₹8 lakh  
- Full-time regular student  
- Not availing another central government scholarship  

**Apply at:** `scholarships.gov.in`

---

## 4. Post Matric Scholarship for SC Students

**Amount:** Tuition reimbursement + maintenance allowance  
**Who it is for:** SC students in healthcare and allied health courses  

### Eligibility
- Scheduled Caste category  
- Indian national  
- Family income below ₹2.5 lakh  
- Enrolled in recognized institution  

This scholarship also supports:
- Hostel residents  
- Day scholars  

**Apply at:** `scholarships.gov.in`

---

## 5. Post Matric Scholarship for ST Students

**Amount:** Tuition reimbursement + maintenance allowance  
**Who it is for:** ST students in MBBS and allied health courses  

### Eligibility
- Scheduled Tribe category  
- Family income below ₹2.5 lakh  
- Enrolled in recognized institution  

This scheme is highly valuable because tuition reimbursement often has **no cap**.

**Apply at:** `scholarships.gov.in`

---

## 6. Prime Minister Scholarship Scheme (PMSS)

**Amount:**  
- ₹3,000/month (male scholars)  
- ₹3,250/month (female scholars)

**Who it is for:** Wards/widows of ex-servicemen, paramilitary personnel, and police  

### Eligibility
- Ward or widow of eligible personnel  
- Minimum 60% in Class 12  
- Enrolled in first year of professional degree  
- Not availing another scholarship  

**Apply at:** `ksb.gov.in`

---

## 7. Infosys Foundation STEM Stars Scholarship

**Amount:** Up to ₹50,000/year  
**Total:** Up to ₹2,00,000  

**Who it is for:** Female students in MBBS, BDS, B.Pharm, and engineering  

### Eligibility
- Female student  
- Enrolled in NIRF-accredited institution  
- Family income ≤ ₹8 lakh  
- Schooling from government/government-aided school  
- Age:
  - ≤19 years (1st year)  
  - ≤20 years (2nd year)

---

## 8. North Eastern Council Merit Scholarship

**Amount:** ₹30,000/year  

**Who it is for:** MBBS students from North Eastern Region  

### Eligibility
- Domicile of any of the 8 North Eastern states  
- Valid NEET scorecard  
- Enrolled in MBBS at NAAC-certified institution  

**Apply at:** `scholarships.gov.in`

---

## 9. Swami Dayanand Education Foundation Scholarship

**Amount:** Up to ₹1 lakh/year  

**Who it is for:** First-year MBBS and B.Tech students  

### Eligibility
- First-year student only  
- Minimum 80% in Class 12  
- Annual family income below ₹12 lakh  
- Cleared NEET/JEE in same year  
- No drop year allowed  

Preference given to government school students.

**Apply at:** `swamidayanand.org`

---

## 10. Federal Bank Hormis Memorial Foundation Scholarship

**Amount:** 100% reimbursement of tuition and educational expenses  

**Who it is for:** First-year MBBS and B.Sc. Nursing students in six states  

Eligible states:
- Gujarat  
- Karnataka  
- Kerala  
- Maharashtra  
- Punjab  
- Tamil Nadu  

### Eligibility
- Resident of eligible state  
- Enrolled in first-year MBBS or B.Sc. Nursing  
- Merit-based admission only  

**Apply at:** `federalbank.co.in`

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Which is the best scholarship for MBBS students in India?

The **GSK Scholars Programme** offers one of the highest corporate scholarship amounts at **₹1,00,000/year for 4.5 years**.

For government schemes, **Post Matric Scholarship for SC/ST students** offers the highest value due to full tuition reimbursement.

---

## Can nursing and pharmacy students apply for the same scholarships as MBBS students?

Not always.

- GSK → MBBS only  
- Central Sector, Post Matric, Infosys STEM Stars, PMSS → broader eligibility  

Always verify course eligibility.

---

## Is there a scholarship for MBBS students in private healthcare colleges?

Yes.

Examples:
- Central Sector Scheme  
- ONGC Scholarship  
- Post Matric SC/ST  
- PMSS  

Some schemes like GSK are government-college-only.

---

## Do I need a NEET rank to apply for healthcare scholarships?

For MBBS-specific scholarships: **Yes**

For nursing/pharmacy scholarships: Often **No**

---

## Can I hold a scholarship and a fee concession together?

Sometimes.

Government schemes often allow both if:
- Fee concession is institutional  
- Scholarship is central/state funded  

Check terms carefully.

---

## When does the next cycle open for 2026-27?

- NSP: Typically opens in **July 2026**
- ONGC: Usually **August–November**
- GSK: Annual cycle for first-year MBBS students
