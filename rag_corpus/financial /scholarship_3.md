# National Scholarship Portal (NSP) for NEET Students: The Ultimate Guide to MBBS & healthcare Scholarships

healthcare education in India can be financially challenging even after clearing NEET UG. While government colleges reduce tuition costs, expenses like hostel fees, textbooks, instruments, and examination fees still add up over the 5.5-year course. Private healthcare colleges and deemed universities can cost anywhere from ₹40 lakh to ₹1 crore+.

The **National Scholarship Portal (NSP)** serves as a centralized platform for scholarships offered by central government ministries, state governments, and corporate sponsors to reduce this burden.

This guide covers scholarships for students pursuing:

- MBBS  
- BDS  
- BAMS  
- BHMS  
- BSMS  
- B.Sc. Nursing  

---

# 1. Top Central Sector NSP Scholarship Schemes for NEET Students

## A. PM-USP Yojana (Formerly CSSS)

**Ministry:** Ministry of Education  

### Target Audience
Meritorious students from low-income families pursuing professional degrees such as MBBS, BDS, and AYUSH.

### Eligibility Criteria
- Must be in the top 80th percentile of successful candidates in Class 12 board exams  
- Annual family income must not exceed ₹4.5 lakh  
- Must be enrolled in a regular full-time undergraduate course at an NMC/DCI-recognized institute  

### Financial Benefits
- ₹12,000/year for first 3 years  
- ₹20,000/year for 4th and 5th years  

---

## B. Top Class Education Scheme for SC/ST Students

**Ministry:**  
- Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment (SC)  
- Ministry of Tribal Affairs (ST)

### Target Audience
SC/ST students admitted into top healthcare colleges through NEET counseling.

### Eligibility Criteria
- Must belong to SC/ST category  
- Valid caste certificate required  
- Admission through NEET counseling  
- Family income cap:
  - SC: ₹8 lakh  
  - ST: ₹6 lakh  

### Financial Benefits
- Full tuition fee coverage  
- Up to ₹2.5 lakh/year in private institutions or actual fee in government institutions  
- Monthly living allowance (~₹2,220)  
- Annual book allowance ₹1,500  
- Laptop grant up to ₹45,000  

---

## C. Merit-Cum-Means (MCM) Scholarship

**Ministry:** Ministry of Minority Affairs  

### Target Audience
Students from minority communities:
- Muslim  
- Christian  
- Sikh  
- Buddhist  
- Parsi  
- Jain  

### Eligibility Criteria
- Minimum 50% in Class 12  
- Admission through NEET rank list  
- Annual parental income below ₹2.5 lakh  

### Financial Benefits
- Fee reimbursement up to ₹20,000/year  
- Maintenance allowance:
  - Hostellers: ₹1,600/month  
  - Day scholars: ₹750/month  
- Paid for 10 months/year

# 4. Step-by-Step Guide to Applying on NSP

## Step 1: One-Time Registration (OTR)

- Visit the National Scholarship Portal  
- Click **New Registration**  
- Verify using Aadhaar + OTP  
- Get OTR ID  

---

## Step 2: Profile & Institute Mapping

Fill:
- Academic details  
- NEET rank  
- College information  

Select your healthcare college from NSP’s institutional database.

---

## Step 3: Scheme Selection & Document Upload

Based on:
- State  
- Income  
- Category  
- NEET score  

NSP shows eligible schemes.

Upload all required documents.

---

## Step 4: Institute Verification

- Review application  
- Download Bonafide Student Certificate  
- Get signature/seal from college Nodal Officer  
- Re-upload certificate  
- Final submit

# 5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

## Double-Dipping

You cannot claim multiple government scholarships in the same academic year.

Example:
- Central Scholarship + State Scholarship = Not allowed  

---

## Aadhaar Seeding Issues

Scholarships are disbursed through DBT.

If bank account is not Aadhaar-seeded:
- Payment failure  
- Delays  
- Scholarship rejection  

---

## Incorrect College Selection

If your college isn’t listed:
- Do not choose a similar college  
- Contact administration immediately  

They need to update college details in NSP backend.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can I take an education loan and an NSP scholarship together?

Yes.  
You can combine both.

Scholarship funds can help with:
- Hostel fees  
- Books  
- Loan repayment  

---

## Is attendance required for renewal?

Yes.

Typically required:
- Minimum 75% attendance  
- Passing marks (usually 50%+)  
- No active backlogs  

Renewal is annual.

---

## Can I change my scholarship scheme after final submission?

No.

After final submission, the application is locked.

If a correction is needed:
- Contact college Nodal Officer  
- Request defect/correction before deadline  

---

# Final Advice

Clearing NEET gets you into healthcare college. Funding your education requires planning.

Apply early, prepare documents carefully, and verify every detail before submission.

Even if government scholarships don’t work out, many private and CSR scholarships can help bridge the gap.
